Security & Privacy Whitepaper

Institutional-grade security, privacy-first design

Design Philosophy

  • Privacy-by-design and minimal data exposure
  • Decision-support only (no enforcement)
  • Institution-controlled deployment and access

Data Handling

  • No storage of personally identifiable information (PII)
  • No retention of raw transactions or content
  • Processing limited to behavioral signals and metadata

Security Controls

  • Encrypted data-in-transit (TLS)
  • Token-based API authorization
  • Environment isolation for engine core

Audit & Governance

  • Audit-ready outputs and logs
  • Institution-defined retention policies
  • Human-in-the-loop interpretation
Important: Veritas Engine does not perform surveillance, identity resolution, or legal classification. All conclusions remain with the deploying institution.

Pattern-Chaining & Risk Graph Intelligence

Pattern-chaining is a core capability of Veritas Engine that links transactions, wallets, and behavioral events over time to construct a probabilistic risk graph.

  • Temporal sequencing of financial events
  • Multi-node wallet relationship graphs
  • Risk propagation across transaction chains
  • Confidence-weighted risk escalation

This approach enables institutions to identify complex fraud and money-laundering risks while maintaining strict privacy and non-surveillance boundaries.
